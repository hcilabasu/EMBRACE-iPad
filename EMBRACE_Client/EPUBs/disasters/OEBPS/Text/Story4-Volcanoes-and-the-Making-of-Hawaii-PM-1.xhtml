Volcanoes and the Making of Hawai'i



Volcanoes spew out hot, melted rock from within the earth.

The melted rock is called lava.

Volcanoes are often found where plates meet, but sometimes a hotspot lets lava spew up in the middle of a plate.

The islands of Hawai'i were formed by the Pacific plate moving over a hotspot.

The lava moves up the plume from the mantle deep in the earth.
