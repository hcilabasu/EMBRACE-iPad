The right atrium is the blue part at the top of the heart.

The blood with carbon dioxide goes into the right atrium of the heart.

When the right atrium is full of blood, the blood pushes through a valve.

Now the blood is in the right ventricle.

The valve closes so that the blood does not go back to the atrium.

The right ventricle squeezes hard,

and that pushes the blood toward the lungs.

In the lungs, the carbon dioxide leaves the blood and goes into the lungs.

The carbon dioxide goes into the air when you breathe out.

The oxygen that you breathe in goes into the blood.

The blood with oxygen goes back to the left atrium.

When the heart beats again, the blood with oxygen will go to the muscles.
