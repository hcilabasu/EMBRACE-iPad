Cigarette smoke hurts your lungs in many ways.

Sometimes we breathe little bits of black dirt and dust in the air.

The dirt gets trapped in the white hairs called cilia.

The cilia push the dirt out of the lungs.

Cigarette smoke kills the cilia so dirt stays in your lungs.

Cigarette smoke also hurts the pink air sacs and makes them stiff and black.

When this happens, it is hard for oxygen to get to the blood.

When there is less oxygen in the blood, your heart and lungs have to work harder.

There is good news!

When people stop smoking, the body cleans the cilia and the air sacs.
