When the heart beats, blood rushes into the arteries to the body.

The blood even goes all the way to the muscles in the arms.

Muscles use food and oxygen from the blood to make energy.

The oxygen in the blood goes into the muscle.

Carbon dioxide is left over from making energy.

The carbon dioxide from the muscle goes into the blood.

In the muscles, the arteries connect to veins. Veins are the little blue tubes.

The blood with carbon dioxide goes into the veins.

When the heart beats, it pushes the blood with carbon dioxide through the veins and back to the heart.
