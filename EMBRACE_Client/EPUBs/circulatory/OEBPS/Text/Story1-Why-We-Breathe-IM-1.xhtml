Why do we breathe?

The air we breathe has different types of molecules.

Oxygen molecules look like two red balls. Each molecule has two atoms of oxygen.

Carbon dioxide molecules looks like a black ball between two red balls. There is one black carbon atom and two red oxygen atoms.

When we breathe in, oxygen in the air goes into the nose.

Then the oxygen goes into the lungs.

There are little tubes in the lungs called arteries, and there is blood in the arteries.

Carbon dioxide from the blood goes into the lungs.

When we breathe out, the carbon dioxide goes into the air.

At the same time, the oxygen goes into the blood.

The blood with oxygen goes back to the heart.

Breathing is how we get oxygen into the blood and move carbon dioxide into the air.

The oxygen helps to turn food into energy so that we can live.
