The heart pumps blood to and from the lungs and then to the whole body. To do this, it has a lot of parts.

The left atrium is the red part of the heart above the white valve.

Blood with oxygen from the lungs goes into the left atrium.

When the atrium is full of blood, the white valve at the bottom of the atrium opens.

The blood flows into the left ventricle.

Next, the valve closes so that the blood cannot go back to the atrium.

Finally, the left ventricle squeezes hard.

The blood rushes out and into the arteries.

When the left ventricle squeezes hard it makes a heart beat.

Sometimes you can feel your heart beat by putting your hand on your chest.

Your heart beats about 60 or 70 times each minute.

Heart beats tell us that a person is alive and pumping blood to the body.
